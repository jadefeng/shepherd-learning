## Essential Key Terms

**ANSI (American National Standards Institute)**  

**Authorized Person**  
An individual assigned by an employer to perform a specific duty or to be present at a particular job site.

**Competent Person**  
An individual who has authorization to take corrective action and can recognize existing and predictable hazards.

**Qualified Person**  
An expert with recognized credentials, knowledge, training, and experience who can solve problems related to the subject matter, work, or project.

## Lesson 1: General Safety and Health Provisions

### Key Points

To reduce the risk of workplace accidents and injuries:

- Employers must conduct frequent and regular inspections of job sites, materials, and equipment.
- The use of machinery, tools, materials, or equipment that do not comply with OSHA standards is prohibited.
- Unsafe equipment must be clearly identified by tagging or locking controls to make them inoperable, or removed from service entirely.
- Employers are responsible for educating and training employees to recognize and avoid unsafe workplace conditions and to control or eliminate hazards that could cause illness or injury.
- In areas where harmful plants or animals are present, employees must be educated on potential hazards, injury prevention, and first aid procedures.
- Combustible scrap and debris must be removed regularly, and safe means must be provided to facilitate removal.
- First aid supplies must be readily accessible when required.
  - First aid kits must be stored in weatherproof containers with individually sealed packages.
  - Employers must inspect first aid kits before each job and at least weekly during active work to ensure supplies are replaced.
- Employees required to enter confined or enclosed spaces must be instructed on:
  - The nature of the hazards  
  - Necessary precautions  
  - Required protective and emergency equipment
- Employers are responsible for ensuring the availability of fire protection and fire suppression equipment.
- Containers must be provided for collecting and separating waste, trash, and other refuse, and must be emptied at frequent and regular intervals.

## Lesson 2: Employee Medical and Exposure Records, Emergency Action Plans, and Means of Egress

### Key Points

- Employees must be provided access to medical and exposure records in a reasonable manner.
- If access cannot be provided within **15 days** of a request, the employer must:
  - State the reason for the delay  
  - Provide the earliest date the records will be available
- Employers must provide employees or their designated representatives access to exposure records upon request.
  - If no records exist, records of employees with similar job duties must be provided.
  - Written consent from other employees is not required.
- Exposure records must reasonably identify:
  - The identity of the hazardous substance  
  - The amount of exposure  
  - The nature of the toxic substance or harmful physical agent
- A physician representing the employer may disclose information about terminal illnesses or psychiatric conditions only to the employee’s designated representative, not directly to the employee.
- Employees must be informed of the existence, location, and availability of medical and exposure records:
  - At the time of initial employment  
  - At least annually thereafter
- Employers must inform employees of their rights under the access standard and make copies available.
- Employees must be told who is responsible for maintaining and providing access to records.
- When an employer ceases business operations:
  - Medical and exposure records must be transferred to a successor employer, if one exists.
  - If no successor exists, affected employees must be notified of their access rights at least **three months** before business closure.
  - The Director of the National Institute for Occupational Safety and Health must be notified in writing at least **three months** before records are disposed of.
- Emergency action plans must define:
  - Types of evacuation procedures  
  - Employee alarm systems  
  - Distinct signals for different emergency purposes
- Exits must be clearly marked with visible signage.
- Means of egress must be continuously maintained and kept free and unobstructed while buildings are occupied.
- Employers must review emergency procedures with employees upon initial assignment.
- Written emergency action plans must be kept at the workplace and available for employee review.
